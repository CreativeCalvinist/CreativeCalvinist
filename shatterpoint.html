Legends of the Galaxy: Star Wars Book Reviews

Vector Prime

Author: R.A. Salvatore

Release Date: October 5, 1999

Series: The New Jedi Order

Era: New Republic Era

Timeline: 25 ABY

★★★★☆ 4/5 Stars
⚠️ Spoiler Warning: This review contains significant plot details.

Review

R.A. Salvatore's "Vector Prime" marks a pivotal moment in Star Wars Legends continuity, launching the ambitious New Jedi Order series with a story that forever changed the landscape of the Star Wars Expanded Universe.

Plot Overview

Set 21 years after the Battle of Yavin, "Vector Prime" introduces the terrifying Yuuzhan Vong - extra-galactic invaders who exist outside the Force and use biological technology. As the New Republic faces this unprecedented threat, our heroes must adapt to an enemy unlike anything they've encountered before.

Strengths

The novel excels in establishing the dire threat of the Yuuzhan Vong, creating a palpable sense of dread throughout. Salvatore's action sequences are particularly noteworthy, especially the space battles that showcase both the tactical ingenuity of our heroes and the alien nature of their adversaries.

Weaknesses

Some readers might find the pacing uneven, particularly in the early chapters as multiple plot threads are established. The scientific explanations for the Yuuzhan Vong's biological technology occasionally feel overwrought.

Impact on the Expanded Universe

This novel represents a dramatic shift in tone for Star Wars literature, introducing consequences and stakes that would ripple through the entire Expanded Universe. Its impact on character development, particularly for the next generation of Jedi, cannot be overstated.
